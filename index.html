About Me

Growing up, I was always dissecting old electronic toys and putting them together to create new ones; robots, in particular, were always my favorite ones to play with. I've always known that engineering is one of my passions, but I wasn't sure which field specifically holds my interest.
Naturally, I attended a university with a well-known engineering department. My bachelor degree was in Electrical Engineering, working mostly with physical electrical components, and what I thought would be my career path until retirement. During my first professional job however, I realized that my true interest lies not so much in the act of putting a system together, but in the endless possibilities of application that the system could be used for.
I decided to pivot my career to be more application-oriented. I went back to school to expand my knowledge to include general computer science, and later on, robotics-specific knowledge; both to improve my capability as an engineer and to satify my curiosity in higher level concepts and ideas in robotics.
This decision paved the way for me to establish a career in the robotics field. I am extremely lucky to be able to work in my chosen field, with iconic robotics companies and world-class engineers as colleagues. I plan to continue my education, as there is always more to learn; plus, I am chronically curious.
